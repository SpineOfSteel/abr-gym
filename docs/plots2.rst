Plots and Evaluation
====================

This page documents the transport-wise comparison figures generated by ``plot_grouped.py`` for the Norway mobile bandwidth dataset. The script groups logs by transport mode, parses each scheme log, computes per-trace summary metrics, and emits one set of comparison plots for the aggregate benchmark plus one set for each transport in ``TRANSPORTS = ['tram', 'car', 'bus', 'ferry', 'metro', 'train']``. The evaluated schemes are ``bb``, ``bola``, ``mpc``, ``rl``, ``ppo``, and ``netllm``, rendered in the legends as **BBA**, **BOLA**, **RobustMPC**, **Pensieve**, **Pen-PPO**, and **NetLLM**.

   :local:
   :depth: 2

Dataset Background
------------------

The traces come from the MMSys 2013 Norway mobile streaming dataset. The dataset organizes measurements by route and transport type, and also provides route maps and observed bandwidth profiles. Measurements were collected during TCP streaming sessions over Telenor's 3G/HSDPA network in Norway using fixed 2-second video segments. Some routes include very weak-signal regions, including tunnel-heavy metro segments, which helps explain abrupt bitrate drops, rebuffer spikes, and large QoE differences across controllers.

The most useful reading pattern for this page is:

#. the **route map**,
#. the **measured bandwidth profile** along that route,
#. the **scheme outcome plots** for bitrate, smoothness, stall, QoE, and bitrate-vs-stall tradeoff.

How to Read the Plots
---------------------

Average bitrate per trace
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

The bitrate plot shows the mean delivered video bitrate for each trace. Higher is only better when it is achieved without frequent stalls or violent switching.

Bitrate smoothness per trace
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

The smoothness plot measures the mean absolute bitrate switch magnitude, i.e. the mean of ``|delta bitrate|``. Lower values indicate a more stable experience.

Time spent on stall per trace
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

The stall plot shows the fraction of time spent rebuffering. Lower is better, and large spikes correspond to severe playback failures.

QoE CDF
~~~~~~~

The QoE CDF is the best single ranking view. Curves further to the right indicate stronger per-trace reward over more of the benchmark.

Bitrate vs stall tradeoff
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

The tradeoff plot places **mean bitrate** on the x-axis and **mean stall ratio** on the y-axis, with error bars showing interquartile spread. The most desirable region is **bottom-right**: high bitrate with low stall. These plots make clear why bitrate alone is not enough to judge an ABR algorithm. Two schemes can have similar mean bitrate, but one may reach it by accepting much higher rebuffer risk.

Combined Results Across All Transports
--------------------------------------

The aggregate ``all`` plots combine every transport trace. They show which controller stays reliable across mixed mobility rather than excelling only on one easier route family.

   :width: 85%
   :align: center

   Mean video bitrate across the full trace set.

Across all transports, bitrate means are close: BBA and RobustMPC are highest at about 1.13 Mbps, followed by Pen-PPO, Pensieve, BOLA, and NetLLM. This already hints that bitrate alone is a weak discriminator.

.. figure:: ../PLOT/graphs/baselines-all-sr.png
   :alt: Bitrate smoothness across all transport traces
   :width: 85%
   :align: center

   Bitrate smoothness across the full trace set.

Smoothness separates the schemes more clearly. NetLLM and Pen-PPO are the smoothest overall, while BBA and BOLA make larger switches.

.. figure:: ../PLOT/graphs/baselines-all-st.png
   :alt: Stall burden across all transport traces
   :width: 85%
   :align: center

   Time spent on stall across the full trace set.

The stall plot explains why BBA's bitrate lead does not translate into the best experience. BBA has the highest stall burden, while Pen-PPO and BOLA are much safer.

.. figure:: ../PLOT/graphs/baselines-all-qoe.png
   :alt: QoE CDF across all transport traces
   :width: 95%
   :align: center

   QoE CDF across the full trace set.

The aggregate QoE CDF ranks Pen-PPO first, with RobustMPC and Pensieve next. BBA is clearly left-shifted despite its high bitrate.

Implementation Notes from ``plot_grouped.py``
---------------------------------------------

Several implementation details directly affect how these figures should be interpreted:

- ``collect_scheme_metrics()`` searches the trace root recursively for ``*.txt`` files and filters transport-specific runs by matching ``_{transport}_`` in the filename stem. It also sorts files by the trailing numeric token in the filename so trace ordering is stable across schemes. 
- The per-scheme accumulator is initialized as ``{'reward': [], 'bitrate': [], 'stall': [], 'smoothness': []}``, so every plot is ultimately driven by per-trace summary values rather than raw per-chunk sequences.
- ``parse_log_file()`` reads four key pieces of information from each line: timestamp, bitrate, rebuffer duration, and reward. Bitrate is converted from kbps to Mbps immediately with ``float(sp[1]) / 1000.0``.
- Malformed rows are skipped with ``try/except``, which makes the parser tolerant to noisy logs but also means partial files can silently contribute fewer samples.
- ``summarize_metrics()`` drops logs with empty bitrate lists or reward lists of length ``<= 1``. This avoids invalid averages and also means missing or truncated logs are excluded from the aggregate.
- ``mean_reward`` is computed as ``np.mean(reward[1:])`` rather than over the full sequence, so the first reward entry is intentionally omitted.
- ``mean_bitrate`` is the arithmetic mean over the parsed bitrate sequence.
- ``stall_pct`` is normalized as ``sum(rebuffer[1:]) / (VIDEO_LEN * 4.0 + sum(rebuffer[1:])) * 100``. This is important: the stall plot is **not raw stall seconds**, but a normalized stall ratio derived from total playback time plus rebuffer time.
- ``smoothness`` is computed as ``mean(abs(diff(bitrate)))``. It measures average switching magnitude, not switch count.
- ``group_plots()`` writes figures using the naming pattern ``baselines-{suffix}-*.png``, where ``suffix`` is either a transport name or ``all``. That output convention is what drives the figure references used in this documentation.
- The aggregate ``all`` plots use ``skip = 3`` while transport-specific plots use ``skip = 1``. This means the full benchmark line plots are intentionally downsampled for readability, while transport-specific views preserve every available trace point.
- ``plot()`` labels each line with the scheme name plus the mean of the plotted metric, which is why the legends expose direct per-panel metric averages.
- ``plot_qoe_cdf()`` builds QoE curves from a 500-bin histogram and cumulative sum. These are empirical distribution summaries over per-trace reward values, not parametric fits.
- ``fix_axes()`` centralizes styling across figures: axis labels, dashed grid, hidden top/right spines, a three-column legend in the upper right, and optional titles. This is why the panels have a consistent visual style throughout the page.
